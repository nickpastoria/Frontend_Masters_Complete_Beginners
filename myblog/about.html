About Me

Hi!

My name is Nick Pastoria! Welcome to my blog website. I'm a computer scientist and game designer from california! I've been programming for as long as I've had access to a computer.

Early Life

Around 2005 I got access to my first netbook at home and was allowed to play minecraft. From there I was creating mods, port forwarding my router, and hosting servers so all of my friends and I could play. I was interested in almost any subject I could get my hands on though. I remember wanting to be a 'renaissance man.' I learned photoshop and video editing. My friends and I made home movies that I edited together and burned onto CD's. I also learned how to solve a Rubicks cube at this time which would be one of my favorite pass-times for a while. I was fortunate enough that my school had an animation class where I learned how to make hand-drawn animations.

Highschool and Early College

In highschool I joined the robotics team but instead of programming I chose to be in design. Here I learned 3D modelling and CAD. I still programmed, just not for robots. I wish my path could have been simple, but sometimes that's not how things roll. When choosing what college to go to I really wanted to go to Santa Cruz's school of Computer Science and Game Design. My family was not as sold on the program. I filled out applications and a few months later I was accepted to CSUN's school of mechanical enineering. I stayed there for a full year, and even got an aerospace internship after just one semester! Something just felt wrong. I felt like I wasn't in the right space. In my free time at home, and at work I found myself programming whenever I got the chance. At work I learned visual basic so I could make games in Excel when I ran out of work to do so it didn't look like I was goofing off. My computer was pointed right at the big shiny glass door of the CFO's office. One day he complimented by work ethic while I sat there working on a game. At home I was making articficial life simulations in Game Maker.

Community College and Tempest Freerunning

I stayed at the internship for about seven or eight months until it was clear I had overstayed my welcome. Sometime during my internship I picked up a life lesson that forever changed the way I made decisions. I was raised to be "smart." So I always made the choice that made the most sense, but I felt like I wasn't living "my" life. So I started living life based on this mentre: "When making a tough decision assign your two options to the sides of a coin. Flip the coin, and before you see the result you already know which one you want more." I dropped out of CSUN and transferred to my local community college to get my associates in computer science. Well... community college took me a lot longer than I expected. I worked on many cool personal projects while I was there. I ran simulations of monopoly games to see which spaces were the best, I wrote conways game of life in the command line for c. I wrote an unoptimized n-body gravitational simulation in p5.js. I also had to work part time in order to pay for my car. One of my dream jobs was to work at a coach at Tempest Freerunning Academy, a place I had been training at since I was early in highschool. I didn't get hired right away. I had to work at a different gymnastics club first "Le Club Gymnastics". After working there for a year I got my opportunity. The pay was lower, and my position would drop significantly. But I already knew which path I would rather take.

UC Santa Cruz

Fall of 2020 I was accepted to UC Santa Cruz in their Computer Science and Game Design program. I worked online for the first year, but fortunately the school didn't accept any of my programming from CC so I gained an extra year at school. Hooray. My multidisciplinary skills truly shined here. I worked as a student podcaster for the UCSC Slugcast. I got to work on amazing projects while improving my writin, art, and programming skills. I won an award for one of my first game projects at the school "Just Focus". Most of all I had a ton of time to work on and develop my people skills.
